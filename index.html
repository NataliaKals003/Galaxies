The largest galaxies

And curiosities about them

IC 1101

IC 1101 is the largest galaxy in the universe. The galaxy is elliptical to lenticular and appears as a golden yellow hue. The brightest galaxy is located more than 1.04 billion light-years away from Earth

Phoenix Cluster

The Phoenix Cluster is one of the most massive galaxy clusters in the universe. It is located approximately 5.7 billion light-years from Earth. The elliptical galaxy is composed of intracluster medium and dark matter

NGC 262

The NGC 262 is located 202 million light-years from Earth, and it is one of the largest known spiral galaxies. The diameter of the galaxy is approximately 1.3 million light-years.

NGC 4889

The NGC 4889, also known as "Coma B," is a supergiant elliptical galaxy located approximately 308 million light-years from Earth. It was also discovered by the British astronomer Frederick William Herschel I in 1785.

NGC 4874

The NGC 4874, also known as "Coma A," was discovered in 1785 by the British astronomer Frederick William Herschel I. It is located 350 million light-years away from Earth. It is a smooth, ball-shaped galaxy surrounded by a stellar circle.

A2261-BCG

A A2261-BCG situou-se a aproximadamente 3 bilhões de anos-luz da Terra. É elíptica em formato e foi encontrada pelo Telescópio Espacial Hubble. A A2261-BCG tem um diâmetro e um milhão de anos-luz.
